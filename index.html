Building Brighter Futures: Transforming Lives through SCA s High-Impact Tutoring.

What is High-Impact Tutoring?

Tutoring is a form of teaching, one-on-one or in a small group, towards a specific goal. High-impact tutoring leads to substantial learning gains for students by supplementing (but not replacing) students’ classroom experiences. High-impact tutoring responds to individual needs and complements students’ existing curriculum.  For the program to have a positive, sustained impact, researchers agree, schools have to adhere to certain specifications. The pillars of high-impact tutoring are: Frequent (2 to 3 times a week) 30-minute sessions held during the school day One-on-one or small group sessions with no more than 4 students  Professionally-trained tutors  A focus on relationship-building relationships by having students meet with the same tutors each session  High-quality materials that align with classroom content

Who are the people served by SCA?

Families living below the poverty line, struggling to meet basic needs such as food, shelter, and healthcare. Immigrant communities facing language barriers, cultural adjustment issues, and limited access to resources. Minority groups disproportionately affected by systemic inequalities and discrimination. At-risk youth vulnerable to factors such as involvement in juvenile justice systems, gang activity, or substance abuse. Students with learning challenges or disabilities who require additional support and accommodations to succeed academically.
•

Challenges faced by these populations

Low income and financial instability, limiting access to educational resources, extracurricular activities, and academic support services. Lack of stable family structures, with some students coming from single-parent households or facing family instability due to factors such as incarceration, substance abuse, or domestic violence. Transportation barriers, particularly in communities with limited public transportation options, making it difficult for students to attend school regularly or access educational programs outside of their neighborhoods. Low educational attainment among parents and caregivers, resulting in limited support for their children's academic endeavors and a lack of awareness about the importance of education.

What are the results of high-impact tutoring?

High-impact tutoring has been shown to be 20 times more effective than standard tutoring models for math and 15 times more effective for reading. Studies demonstrate that the practice increases students’ learning by an additional three to 15 months across grade levels. The University of Chicago Urban Labs tested Saga’s impact via large-scale randomized controlled trials. They found that: Students learn up to 2.5 years of math in one academic year, and math course failures are reduced by up to 63%. Course failures in non-tutored subjects are reduced by up to 26%. Student attendance increased by as much as 18 days per year. These unparalleled results show that high-impact tutoring is a clear solution for improving educational equity and helping students succeed.
